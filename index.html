Overview of Earth

  • Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life.
  • It has a diverse range of ecosystems, climates, and geological features.
  • Approximately 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water, with the remaining 29% being land.
  • Earth's atmosphere is composed mainly of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), with trace amounts of other gases.
  • Hydrosphere: Earth’s water in liquid form is essential for life. Water is present in oceans, ice caps, rivers, lakes, the atmosphere, and underground.
  • Earth’s interior is hot, leading to dynamic geological activity. Heat from the core escapes mainly along mid-ocean ridges, fueling volcanism and plate tectonics.
  • Human civilization reshapes the surface through agriculture, urbanization, deforestation, and other activities. Much of the habitable land is used for food production, with livestock grazing taking up more area than crops for human food.



Earth's Ocean Coverage: Influence on Climate and Life Support

  • Global Heat Regulation: Oceans cover roughly 71% of Earth’s surface, acting as a massive heat reservoir. They absorb and retain solar energy, preventing extreme temperature fluctuations between day and night or season to season. By redistributing warmth via currents like the Gulf Stream and the Antarctic Circumpolar Current, oceans moderate both regional and global climates, helping to keep large land areas habitable.
  • Weather and Rainfall: The vast surface area of the oceans is the dominant source of Earth’s evaporation, driving the global water cycle. Most cloud formation and precipitation originate from oceanic evaporation, influencing rainfall, droughts, monsoons, and the formation of tropical storms. Weather patterns such as El Niño and La Niña are direct results of fluctuations in ocean temperatures and currents.
  • Carbon Dioxide Buffer: Oceans absorb around a quarter of anthropogenic (human-generated) carbon dioxide emissions. By drawing CO₂ from the atmosphere and storing it as dissolved gas, ocean waters slow the pace of global warming. They also take up excess heat produced by greenhouse gases, reducing the immediate rise in atmospheric temperatures.
  • Mitigation of Natural Disasters: Oceanic thermal inertia weakens extreme weather variations, and the complex interplay between ocean and atmosphere influences the development of major weather systems like hurricanes and cyclones.


  • Life Support and the Biosphere Oxygen Generation Oceanic phytoplankton are responsible for producing more than half of the world’s oxygen through photosynthesis. This forms the base of the marine food web and is critical for supporting all aerobic life, both in the sea and on land
  • Carbon Dioxide Absorption Oceans absorb and store a significant portion of human-generated carbon dioxide emissions, as well as excess heat, which helps slow global warming and climate change impacts on land. This CO₂ uptake helps regulate atmospheric composition, but rising levels can increase ocean acidity, affecting shellfish, coral reefs, and wider marine biodiversity
    • Biodiversity and Food Resources Oceans cover about 70% of Earth’s surface and provide about 95% of the planet’s biosphere, supporting an extraordinary range of species and ecosystems. Marine life forms—from microscopic plankton to large whales—form the foundation for complex food webs, providing food and livelihoods for billions of people globally
  • Coastal Populations and Ecosystems Over 40% of the world's population lives near the coast, relying on the ocean for food, water, trade, and cultural identity. Healthy oceans and coasts are crucial for supporting fisheries, mitigating storm surges, and protecting against coastal eros




Major Human Activities That Harm Life on Earth

  • Habitat Destruction Large-scale deforestation, urbanization, and conversion of land for agriculture eliminate natural habitats, driving species to extinction and disrupting biodiversity
  • Pollution Release of greenhouse gases from industrial activity, as well as the dumping of plastics, chemicals, and other waste, contaminates air, water, and soil, making environments toxic for living organisms
  • Climate Change Excessive emissions of carbon dioxide and methane warm the planet, leading to rising sea levels, more extreme weather, and failing ecosystems, all of which endanger human and non-human life
  • Nuclear Threats and Warfare The existence and proliferation of nuclear weapons pose an existential threat. Accidental or intentional detonation could cause immediate mass casualties and long-term environmental devastation
  • Biological and Chemical Threats:Creation and potential spread of engineered viruses, chemical agents, or biological weapons could lead to pandemics or ecological disasters.



How to Help Save the Earth

  • Reduce, Reuse, Recycle: Minimize waste by reducing consumption, reusing items, and recycling materials whenever possible.
  • Conserve Energy: Use energy-efficient appliances, switch to renewable energy sources, and reduce energy consumption in daily life.
  • Support Sustainable Practices: Choose products from companies that prioritize sustainability and ethical sourcing.
  • Plant Trees: Participate in tree planting initiatives to help restore natural habitats and combat climate change.
  • Educate Others: Share knowledge about environmental issues and encourage others to take action.
  • Support Green Transportation: Walk, bike, or use public transport instead of driving for short trips. Carpool or use electric vehicles where feasible. These actions lower air pollution without harming nature’s balance
